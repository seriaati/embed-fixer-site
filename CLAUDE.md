## Design Context

### Users

- Primary users are Discord server owners and moderators.
- They evaluate tools quickly and need confidence that shared social links will render correctly without extra moderation overhead.
- Core job-to-be-done: preserve conversation flow by fixing broken embeds in-channel so members do not need to leave Discord.

### Brand Personality

- 3-word personality: **Customizable, reliable, useful**.
- Voice and tone should be direct, practical, and reassuring.
- Emotional goal: reduce friction and uncertainty around broken embeds while signaling trustworthiness and technical competence.

### Aesthetic Direction

- Continue the current established visual language as the baseline reference.
- Preferred direction: **minimal + clean**.
- Theme target: **dark mode only** for now.
- Preserve existing strengths: restrained gradients, high legibility, rounded controls, clear hierarchy, and conversion-focused CTAs.
- Avoid directions that feel noisy, gimmicky, or disconnected from the current style.

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity before decoration** — prioritize scanability, concise copy, and obvious hierarchy in every section.
2. **Trust through consistency** — keep interactions and visual patterns predictable, stable, and polished.
3. **Customization should feel approachable** — expose flexibility without increasing cognitive load.
4. **Keep users in Discord** — emphasize in-context continuity and reduced context switching.
5. **Accessibility by default (pragmatic)** — maintain strong contrast, clear focus states, and reduced-motion support even without a strict WCAG mandate.
